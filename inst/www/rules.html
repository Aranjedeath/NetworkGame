You have been selected by the President to plan the country's communication network. You must be very smart!

As the country grows and new cities are established they will need to be connected to other cities in the country. Each year a new city will be established. You choose the rules that determine how that new city gets wired into the network.

You will select how much influence each of the following has on the design of your network:

Chance

Should you just roll dice and leave it all to chance? Or should every connection be precisely determined?

Popularity

Should a new city be connected to a city that already has a lot of connections?

Age

Should new cities connect to cities that have been around for a long time?

Be careful! These selections matter. After building your network, it will be scored. The more robust the better. You will start: get a message from A to B. Each round a city will shut-down by chance. The another city. And the next. The longer you network can still get from A to B, the higher your score.

Got it?

At the end you will compare you score to everyone else who has designed a similar network.

Are you up to the task?
